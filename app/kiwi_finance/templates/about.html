Our Story

Kiwi Finance started as a personal project to answer a simple question: where does my money actually go? Most apps give you a surface-level view. We wanted something deeper — automatic syncing, real data exports, and the ability to build your own analysis on top of your own financial data.

We're a small team of engineers and finance nerds who believe that financial transparency shouldn't require a financial advisor. If you can connect a bank account, you should be able to understand your entire financial picture.

How It Works

A simple, powerful pipeline from your bank to your dashboard — and beyond.

1
Connect your bank via Plaid
Use Plaid Link to securely connect any US bank account. Your credentials never touch our servers — Plaid handles authentication and returns a secure access token.
2
Transactions sync automatically
Kiwi Finance uses Plaid's incremental sync API to pull new, modified, and removed transactions daily. A cursor tracks exactly where you left off so nothing is missed or duplicated.
3
Data lands in your local database
All accounts and transactions are stored in a local SQLite database. You own the data. You can query it directly, export it, or build on top of it however you like.
4
Visualize with the dashboard
The built-in dashboard shows spending by day, merchant, and amount bucket. Filter by date range, toggle pending transactions, and drill into exactly what you want to see.
5
Export to S3 for deeper analysis
One click exports your accounts and transactions to Amazon S3 as timestamped CSV files. From there, you can run Athena queries, build Redshift tables, or feed any analytics tool you choose.

Privacy Policy

Kiwi Finance is designed with privacy as a core principle. We do not sell your data. We do not share your financial information with third parties. Your bank credentials are handled entirely by Plaid — we only receive and store the access tokens they provide.

All financial data (accounts, transactions) is stored locally in your own SQLite database and optionally exported to your own S3 bucket. You control where your data lives.
